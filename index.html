I'm Jesse. I'm a support worker based in Melbourne's north. I work with disabled adults who want supports that fit how their brain, lifestyle and interests work.

I am proudly neurodivergent, Deaf, and queer, and I know what it's like to be treated differently. My goal is to provide sensitive and you-centric supports to people with all types of disabilites, and I specialise in supporting people who share my identities.

Neuroaffirming Trans & LGBTQIA+ affirming Self-managed & plan-managed Adults 18+ Coburg / Preston & surrounds

My approach

I practise neuroaffirmingly — which means I'm not trying to make you seem more neurotypical, more socially palatable, or more manageable. I'm working with how you actually are, on the days you actually have. That includes bad days, no-words days, can't-leave-the-house days. None of that is a problem to fix.

I'm trans-affirming and LGBTQIA+ inclusive. I'll use your correct name and pronouns without being asked twice, I'm familiar with the way disability and queer identities can intersect, and you don't need to explain or justify yourself to me.

I bring some of my own neurodivergent experience to this work. I have ADHD and I move in autistic communities. I'm not claiming shared experience is the same as yours — but I genuinely understand a lot of what you might be navigating, and I'm not going to look at you like you need to explain why something is hard.

I'm flexible and low-pressure. Life is irregular, especially when you're disabled. Rescheduling is okay with reasonable notice — I'd rather you tell me you're struggling than push through something that's not working. I'll always be honest if something isn't working from my side too.

I'm also honest about what I can't do. I don't provide hands-on personal care, clinical or health-related support, or work with people under 18. I'll always tell you clearly if something is outside my scope rather than take the work and do it badly.
